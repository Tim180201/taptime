# EV-0001: NFC-confirmed Time Tracking Hypothesis

Status: Potential Evidence  
Date: 2026-06-26  
Source: Human Architect product input during Sprint 1

## Observation

The Human Architect described the frogs reference use case:

- A teacher visits a student at home.
- The student has an NFC card.
- The teacher scans the card to start the lesson.
- The teacher scans the card again to stop the lesson.

This reduces manual search effort in the time tracking tool and creates a stronger confirmation moment than a purely manual start/stop action.

## Potential Product Insight

NFC is not only a technical convenience. It can create:

- faster time tracking
- reduced wrong-customer selection
- customer-side participation
- perceived belonging to the service company
- stronger operational evidence for start and stop times

## Current Interpretation

TapTim.e may be positioned not merely as time tracking, but as fast and confirmable work-event tracking for mobile work scenarios.

## Evidence Status

This is not yet validated market evidence.

It is a product hypothesis derived from real project experience and should be validated by the Research Agent through market and competitor research.
